## 1ï¸âƒ£ What is ANOVA?

**ANOVA** (Analysis of Variance) is a statistical method used to:

> Compare the **means of three or more groups**  
> to check whether **at least one group mean is significantly different**.

Instead of comparing means directly, ANOVA compares **variations (variances)**.

---

## 2ï¸âƒ£ Why ANOVA is Used

- To compare **3 or more groups**
- To avoid many t-tests  
- To reduce **Type I error**  
- To study treatment effects

## 4ï¸âƒ£ Hypotheses in ANOVA (Clearly Explained)

Before performing ANOVA, we set two hypotheses.

### ðŸ”¹ Null Hypothesis (Hâ‚€)

\[

**Meaning:**

This means:
- There is **no real difference** between the groups  
- Any difference is due to **random chance**  
- The treatment has **no significant effect**

**Example:**  
If three teaching methods give similar marks, we assume:  
> All methods are equally effective.

---

### ðŸ”¹ Alternative Hypothesis (Hâ‚)

\[

\]

**Meaning:**  
At least one group mean is **not equal** to the others.

This means:
- A **real difference exists**  
- The treatment **has an effect**  
- Not all groups perform the same  

âš  ANOVA does **not** tell us *which* group is different â€”

## ðŸ” Decision Rule

- If **F-value is small** â†’ Accept \(H_0\)  
- If **F-value is large** â†’ Reject \(H_0\), accept \(H_1\)
